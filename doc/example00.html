Examples of using ONTO-PERL

  • Finding and getting elements (e.g. terms, relationships)
    1. Find all the terms in a given ontology.
    2. Find all the relationships in a given ontology.
    3. Find all the terms using a regular expression for the ID.
    4. Get a specific term by name.
    5. Get a specific term by ID.
  • Getting children terms and/or descendant ones
    1. Get all the descendant terms of a given term.
    2. Get only the child terms of a given term.
  • Getting parent terms and/or ancestor ones
    1. Get all the ancestor terms of a given term.
    2. Get the only the parent terms of a given term.
  • Intersection of ontologies
    1. Find out the intersection of two given ontologies o1 and o3.
  • Getting a term by its features (e.g. synonyms, xref)
    1. Get a term by its name or any of its synonyms.
    2. Get a term by one of its xref's.
  • Working with obsolete terms
    1. Get all the obsolete terms in a given ontology.
  • Basic metrics in an ontology
    1. Get the total number of terms and relationships in a given ontology.
  • Union of ontologies
    1. Find out the union of two given ontologies o1 and o4.
  • Working with sub-ontologies
    1. Building an ontology from an existing sub-ontology.
  • Ontology merging
    1. Simple merging of several and disjoint OBO ontologies from the same IDspace.
  • Manipulating Ontology Terms
    1. Manipulating Ontology Terms (names, IDs, xref's, definitions, and so forth).
  • Working with an Ontology
    1. Manipulating an Ontology.
    2. Getting sub-ontologies from a given ontology.
    3. Get paths from a given term A to a term B and/or from A to a set of given terms.
  • Exporting ontologies into different formats
    1. Export a given ontology to OWL format (oboInOwl mapping).
    2. Export a given ontology to GML format.
    3. Export a given ontology to XML format.
    4. Export a given ontology to DOT format.
    5. Export a given ontology to OBO format (spec 1.2).
    6. Export a given ontology to RDF format.
